Clean Water, Healthy Lives

Why Clean Water Matters

Access to safe water saves lives. Millions still suffer from preventable diseases caused by unsafe water. Together, we can make a difference. Access to clean water and sanitation is fundamental to human dignity, health, and development. Health Impact: Contaminated water and poor sanitation are linked to diseases like cholera, diarrhea, and typhoid. In Uganda alone, diarrheal diseases kill dozens of children daily. Gender Equity: Women and girls are disproportionately affected, especially in areas without private sanitation facilities.

Helping people bring water, sanitation, and hygiene to their communities

WATER

Our teams constructs piped water supply systems in local areas, and uses a Water as a Business model to make sure water services are sustainable. This type of model uses the private sector to manage water systems, and the approach creates jobs for water point mechanics, private entrepreneurs, and water kiosk and hand pump attendants. We help train these entrepreneurs to manage and repair water systems.

SANITATION AND HEALTH

Our team invent and test new sanitation products and technologies that are designed to meet Ugandan communities sanitation needs. We also support sanitation businesses like masons and pit latrine emptiers, connecting them to new Sanitation Hub technologies and providing them with business support.

SCHOOL AND HYGIENE

Our team trains teachers and school health clubs on proper water, sanitation, and hygiene practices, as well as menstrual hygiene management. We have also begun installing rainwater catchment systems in schools for water reuse.
